Legal and Ethical considerations within Vechicle Forensics.

A look at legal considerations when extracting and analysing vehicle data.

Introduction

In this section we will look at best practices for dealing with forensic evidence originating from vehicles, as well as other important considerations to make.

ACPO Principles for Digital Evidence Handling

The ACPO principles are a set of guidelines for handling computer based evidence, provided by the Association of Chief Police Officers. Here we will look at these rules, and how we can apply them to vehicle forensics.

  • 1. No action must be taken that will change data held on a digital device that could later be relied on as evidence in Court.
    • • Always use a write blocker when extracting data from a device.
    • • Take pictures of the vehicle's dash and ports before engaging with any evidence.
  • 2. If it's necessary to access original data, you must be competent to do so and able to explain your actions and their impact.
    • • Use tools with published accuracy rates.
    • • Prove tools' effectiveness on practice datasets before casework.
  • 3. A record of all actions applied to digital evidence must be created and preserved.
    • • Use chain of custody forms for all evidence, documenting every step.
    • • Record SHA-256 hashes before/after extraction where possible.
  • 4. The lead investigator has overall responsibility for ensuring these principles are followed.
    • • Conduct regular audits of team compliance with these standards.
    • • Maintain certification records for all forensic tools used.
