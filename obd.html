On-Board Diagnostics (OBD) Forensics

What is OBD and why is it important?

On-Board Diagnostics refers to a collection of system data that is stored about a vehicle, often used for monitoring the health of an engine and identifying issues that may be occurring. This data can provide valuable insights when investigating a range of different scenarios. We will discuss some of these below:

  • Accident Investigation: OBD information can help to piece together the a situation before a crash occured
  • Fraud Investigations: OBD data can help to differentiate staged accidents.
  • Vehicle Theft Analysis: Logs unauthorized access attempts or key reprogramming.
  • Mechanical Failure Diagnosis: Identifies engine faults or brake failures.

2009-2011 Toyota Acceleration Recalls

In the late 2010s, Toyota were forced to recall approximately 7.5million vehicles after reports that vehicles were unintendingly acceleratoring. Initially, Toyota believed the problem was solely down to floor mats that hid slid forward, and caused the accelerator pedal to become stuck. Whilst this was correct, and led to over 5 million vehicles being recalled, they had a different issue on their hands.

On the 28th of August, 2009, in San Diego California, a passenger of a Toyota Lexus sedan rang 911 and stated that they were acceleratoring uncontrollably. This resulted in them crashing, and all four occupants of the vehicle died. Upon inspection of the vehicle, it was found that the floor mats had been stored in the boot, in the advice of Toyota. This meant they had a new problem. Investigators of the crash used OBD data to confirm that the vehicle had been acceleratoring fully, but also braking. This confirmed to them that there was a problem with the accelerator pedal. Unexpectedly, a team from NASA investigated the so called "sticky pedal" problem, but found no software issues. During investigations, it was found that this problem had led to 90 deaths. In the worst case scenario, when a pedal is pushed to a specific settings, it stays at that level of power even if the driver removes their foot from the pedal.

Toyota were taken to court, and resulted in paying over $1.2 for allegedly concealing information about flawed pedals, the largest penalty imposed on an automaker from the US Justice Department at the time.
